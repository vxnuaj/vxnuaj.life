***If you can identify when you’re complacent, and then take action to change it, it’s such a frickin superpower.***

This. It really is a superpower. It’s how you can stay consistent. When you see dips in your life, in how you’re performing, in your energy levels, in your health, in your habits, in your mentality, you have to take action to change it. 

If there’s initial internal resistance, take action anyways. You’ll feel better afterwards. And you’ll see how your situation is better afterwards. Given that proper action in the right direction was taken.

The feeling of complacency will then cease to exist. If you take action to change what makes you complacent you’ll find it easier and simpler to remain driven, consistent, ambitious, and motivated. 

You build the habit of identifying when you’re complacent, you build the habit of taking the action to fix it, and you’ll stay consistent with what you do. And then whatever you do will compound into greater results.

If you’re complacent then how will you make progress? 

*********************************************Not feeling good will just compound downwards.*********************************************

It’s important to keep this in mind. Everything we do with consistency will compound. Whether we realize it or not. It’s how habits and thought patterns form. 

If you embody a positive mindset, positivity will compound. Probably into a more positive and brighter view of your life.

If you embody a negative mindset, negativity will compound. Probably into a more negative and pessimistic view of your life.

If you don’t take action to change a negative mindset, a negative view, a negative situation, and it continues on with consistency, instead of gradually improving 1% every day, you’ll instead deteriorate 1% every day. 

It’s important to identify what makes you feel good, whether it be the sun, family and friends, nature, drawing, writing, journaling, exercise, and you’ll be able to use those tools to your advantage. 

What’s important is action.

And it’ll compound. 

Then overtime you feel consistently good, you’ll consistently have a positive mindset, and you’ll feel consistently better.

There will always be dips. The highs of life and the lows of life. It’s natural. And let it be natural

But if you constantly feel bad, it’s important you take action.

******************************************************************************It takes activation energy******************************************************************************

It takes activation energy to get out of complacency. It might be a lot. Or it might not be. 

You might face initial internal resistance. You might be lazy at first.

**Keep this in mind:** You’ll be in a better position afterwards. And if you’ll be in a better position, then why would you not want to spend that energy for yourself? 

Take going to the gym for example. There are times when some people might not feel like going to the gym. 

But after they spend the initial energy to get up and going, they’ll feel great throughout the workout. They’ll feel better.

And even in the unlikely scenario that they disliked the gym, it was still worth it. They took action and did something hard that made them uncomfortable. They practiced being [antifragile](https://resiliencei.com/blog/antifragile-should-you-aspire-to-it). 

In the end it’s worth spending that energy.

******************************************************************************Ambition and Satisfaction******************************************************************************

Can ambition coexist with feeling satisfied in life? Can you be satisfied and completely happy with where you are in life while staying ambitious to improve?

Here’s my personal take on this: 

No you can’t be satisfied and completely happy with your current state of life while having ambition. 

You’ll feel moments of satisfaction and happiness when you achieve a goal but it goes away. So then you focus on accomplishing the next goal.
Then, you feel another moment of satisfaction and happiness when you accomplish said goal. But then it goes away. 
And then you begin spiraling into a loop of striving for something, achieving that something, but never feeling truly fulfilled with what you’re doing.

What’s more important is feeling happy and satisfied with what you’re striving towards and how you’re striving towards it. 
It’s about genuinely caring about what you do and genuinely enjoying what you do.

It’s about enjoying the process.
Not doing everything just for the outcome you’ll get.
It’s about being process oriented instead of being goal oriented. (a nice [blog post](https://www.jakeyou.com/blog/process-oriented-vs-goal-oriented-tips#:~:text=Being%20goal%2Doriented%20means%20you,the%20path%20toward%20some%20goal.) explaining this)

The aim may be to accomplish a goal and to be successful. But you won’t be if you don’t enjoy what you do.

Enjoy the fact that you’re ambitious and be grateful that it’s part of yourself. Because that ambition will lead somewhere worthwhile given that you have a proper direction.

***Don’t let positive feedback loops slow you down.***

***********************“the reality is, the people around you are probably giving you so much positive feedback for that one article you did or that one project you did…don’t let that get to you. You should just know that you are capable of way more and continue on the same or faster velocity. Don’t let it slow you down, don’t get complacent, just keep going deeper. Accelerate.”***********************

Staying mindful and aware of the feedback you get from others is a neccessity. If people are telling you, *“this project is really interesting!” or “wow that’s so cool and impressive!”*, you should ask yourself, what do I think of what I did and am I capable of more? When you work on a high level with high standards, what you created might appear impressive to others who aren’t on that high level but to you it might not be actual progress. **What is tall to someone short will be short to someone really tall.** 

Don’t stop leveling up and striving for higher standards just because people give you positive feedback. More than likely, you are capable of more.

And when you embody high standards in your level of work, seek out feedback from people who embody high standards in their level of work. You have to ask good and specific questions on what you want feedback on. In that way, you’ll receive feedback that’ll actually help you make progress. 

*“this project is really interesting!” or “wow that’s so cool and impressive!”* isn’t constructive feedback, it is superficial feedback. You’ll get that if you don’t seek out constructive feedback. Constructive feedback would focus on specific aspect of what you created and would give you specific feedback clearly outlining what can be improved and what was done well.

For example let’s say you wrote an Article.

Superficial feedback: 

- Good job on writing the article!
- Your writing skills are impressive!

Constructive feedback

- The tone of the article doesn’t suit the content. It needs to be presented in a way that is more engaging and understandable to the reader to capture their attention. You can do so by using different words or phrases that can help the reader understand and keep them interested in the article
- I liked the way you structure the article, it was organized in a way that made it easy to read and understand. Delving in deep on the topic wouldn’t have been engaging for the reader without a good background. It’s good that you provided this in the intro of the article.

***************************************************************************************************High Interest and High Difficulty***************************************************************************************************

Here is where good work happens. You’re deeply interested in a topic that challenges you. It’s where you find worthwhile challenge and where you grow your knowledge and skills. You’ll feel good about what you learn.

Low interest High difficulty may not lead to the best work as you aren’t intrinsically interested. Where is the passion and excitement that’ll help you create something wonderful in the midst of challenge? Of course not everything will appear interesting at the start. You have to spend energy understanding and grasping a topic to develop an interest towards it.

Low interest Low difficulty: It isn’t a challenge for you. Or it isn’t remotely interesting. It’s not a place where growth happens and is actually where complacency will begin.

High Interest Low difficulty: Ask yourself, how can I make this more challenging? How can I leverage my high interest to create something interesting? Chances are, if it’s easy, then it’s been done before. Challenging yourself and exploring the unknown will lead to innovation.
